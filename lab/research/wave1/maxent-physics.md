## arXiv/Literature Scout Report: MaxEnt, Belief Propagation, Ising/Potts, RG, Active Search for Deterministic Code Localization

### (1) Maximum-entropy ranking/fusion — query-side constraints, no relevance labels

**Key result:** Iterative Scaling (Darroch-Ratcliff / Improved Iterative Scaling) finds the unique maximum-entropy distribution p*(y|x) satisfying a set of linear feature-expectation constraints, via closed-form multiplicative updates — provably convergent, no gradient descent on labels required ([Cluster Expansions and Iterative Scaling for MaxEnt LMs](https://arxiv.org/pdf/cmp-lg/9509003), [Iterative Scaling and Coordinate Descent Methods](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/220873699_Iterative_Scaling_and_Coordinate_Descent_Methods_for_Maximum_Entropy)). Separately, "Yet Another Method for Combining Classifiers Outputs: A Maximum Entropy Approach" ([Springer](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-540-25966-4_8)) treats *fusion itself* as a MaxEnt problem: each classifier/channel supplies a constraint on the combined distribution (e.g., marginal agreement with that channel's own confidence), and the fused ranking is the max-entropy distribution consistent with all channels simultaneously — the least-committal combination, not a weighted-sum heuristic. The Inverse Square Rank Fusion family is a related fully unsupervised, training-free rank-fusion approach with comparable-or-better effectiveness than supervised fusion baselines ([Inverse square rank fusion](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/269297307_Inverse_square_rank_fusion_for_multimodal_search)).

**One-line leap:** treat each of your 7 evidence channels (BM25 score, import-graph proximity, path similarity, etc.) as a *constraint on a distribution over candidate files*, not a term in a weighted sum — the fusion weights emerge from solving for the max-entropy distribution rather than being hand-tuned.

**Deterministic mechanism sketch:** for each channel c define a feature f_c(file) (e.g., normalized BM25 score) and require E_p[f_c] = observed empirical mean of f_c over the query's channel outputs. Solve for p(file) = (1/Z)·exp(Σ_c λ_c f_c(file)) via Generalized/Improved Iterative Scaling (a few dozen closed-form multiplicative updates on λ_c, no training data, no labels — constraints come from the query's own evidence, exactly the "query-side constraints" framing you asked about). This directly replaces hand-tuned linear-combination weights with a principled, per-query-adaptive, entropy-maximizing combination — and Z and the λ's give you calibrated confidence, useful for a stopping rule. **Target: fusion.**

### (2) Belief propagation / factor graphs on dependency graphs

**Key result:** No paper found does BP specifically for bug/fix localization on file-dependency graphs — this looks like an open gap, not a saturated area. What exists: BP/loopy BP is well-established as local, deterministic (no training) message-passing inference on any factor graph, with fixed points corresponding to stationary points of the Bethe free energy ([Loopy BP: Convergence and Effects](https://jmlr.org/papers/volume6/ihler05a/ihler05a.pdf)); it has been applied to *sensemaking over citation graphs*, explicitly noted for supporting multiple simultaneous entry points and soft clustering ([Synergi](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.07517)) — structurally the same shape as "issue terms enter at several files, fix-probability propagates outward." Adjacent but neural (i.e., trained, not what you want): Neural Enhanced BP on Factor Graphs ([arXiv:2003.01998](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2003.01998)), BP Neural Networks ([arXiv:2007.00295](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.00295)).

**One-line leap:** issue/query terms are observed leaf factors, import/call edges are the graph, fix-probability at each file is the latent variable — loopy BP is a deterministic, few-millisecond alternative to your current propagation heuristic, with a principled convergence diagnostic (message change under a damping threshold) instead of an arbitrary hop-count cutoff.

**Deterministic mechanism sketch:** variable nodes = files, factor nodes = (a) unary evidence factors tying observed query-term matches to each file, (b) pairwise edge factors along import/call/co-change edges encoding "if u is buggy, v is more likely buggy" as a fixed potential (Ising-like, see below). Run 5-20 rounds of damped sum-product; the marginal belief at each file *is* your ranking score, and the message-change norm gives a natural, evidence-based early-stop rule for the propagation depth you're currently hand-tuning. **Target: fusion / within-subsystem.**

### (3) Ising/Potts formulations of module identification

**Key result:** Community detection has a direct, decades-deep lineage from the 2-state Lenz-Ising model to the q-state Potts model, with the Reichardt-Bornholdt Potts formulation and its resolution-limit-free variant ([arXiv:0803.2548](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0803.2548)) giving deterministic energy functions whose ground state (via simulated annealing or spectral relaxation) is the community partition. The 2025 Constant Potts Model paper ([arXiv:2509.03834](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.03834)) reframes CPM as a hedonic (potential) game — every node's local move is guaranteed to (weakly) decrease global energy, giving an anytime-deterministic local-search procedure with a proven Lyapunov/potential function, tested against Leiden/Louvain on LFR benchmarks with comparable modularity at lower overhead.

**One-line leap:** don't just cluster files into subsystems once, offline — use the Potts energy *at query time* with query-relevance as an external field term (a per-node bias, exactly analogous to a magnetic field in the Ising Hamiltonian) so the "ground state" partition is pulled toward putting query-relevant files in the same community, giving you a principled within-subsystem discriminator instead of a static module boundary.

**Deterministic mechanism sketch:** H = -Σ_{edges} J_{uv} δ(s_u,s_v) - Σ_u h_u δ(s_u, s_query) where J_{uv} is edge weight (import/co-change strength) and h_u is the query-relevance field (e.g., BM25 score) pulling node u toward the query's "spin." Local-move (Leiden-style) descent to a local energy minimum is deterministic given a fixed move order and gives a same-community set = your subsystem-restricted candidate pool, replacing a hand-tuned module heuristic with an energy minimization that has convergence guarantees. **Target: within-subsystem.**

### (4) RG-style multiscale coarse-graining

**Key result:** RG-inspired coarse-graining is genuinely being used for search/memory, not just loosely as "hierarchical." RGMem (ICML 2026, [arXiv:2510.16392](https://www.arxiv.org/pdf/2510.16392)) explicitly builds a 3-scale RG-like tower (episodic → semantic fact → user-insight) with thresholded, "phase-transition-like" rescaling between levels, beating SOTA memory-retrieval baselines on LOCOMO/PersonaMem. Renormalization-Group-Guided Tensor Network Structure Search ([arXiv:2512.24663](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.24663)) uses actual block-spin-style decimation to navigate a combinatorial structure-search space coarse-to-fine without exhaustive evaluation — the closest thing found to real RG math (not just "hierarchical indexing") applied to a search problem.

**One-line leap:** define an explicit decimation/coarse-graining operator (block-average of BM25+graph signals over a directory or module = a "block spin") so that coarse-scale search operates on renormalized effective couplings between *modules*, and only the winning coarse block gets refined at file granularity — giving a formal justification (fixed-point of the RG flow = stable module-level relevance) for why coarse→fine pruning doesn't lose recall, rather than an empirically-tuned two-stage heuristic.

**Deterministic mechanism sketch:** partition the repo into blocks (directories/packages); define block-level "coupling" J_block = aggregated edge weight between blocks and block-level field h_block = aggregated query relevance (sum or logsumexp over member files — the natural RG coarse-graining rule for exponential-family scores). Search this reduced system first (cheap), then only decimate back down (refine) within the top-k blocks. This is literally what your lab's 10k-token localization pipeline is already doing operationally (commit `13d33a4`) — RG gives it a formal fixed-point argument and a principled rule for *how much* to coarsen without losing the eventual fine answer. **Target: within-subsystem / @1.**

### (5) Entropy-based query difficulty & active/information-gain probing

**Key result:** Query Performance Prediction (QPP) has an entropy-of-score-distribution variant for neural retrievers ([Entropy-Based QPP](https://ceur-ws.org/Vol-3366/paper-07.pdf)) — a direct, training-free difficulty signal you could use to decide whether to spend more budget reading additional files. Separately, classical Bayesian search theory (Lindley 1956 expected-information-gain; the USS Scorpion search — [Bayesian Search Theory, Duke lab](https://www2.stat.duke.edu/~banks/130-labs.dir/lab10.dir/Lab10_bayesian_search.pdf), and its modern descendant, the Air France AF447 wreckage search ([arXiv:1405.4720](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1405.4720))) gives the exact "which cell to search next to maximize posterior concentration" update rule: after each probe (grid cell / file read), renormalize the posterior over target location by (1 − detection-probability·prior) in unsearched cells, and always probe the cell maximizing expected posterior mass reduction. This is a genuine deterministic, no-training active-search policy with real operational precedent (found 3 lost vessels/aircraft).

**One-line leap:** treat "which file to open next" exactly as AF447/Scorpion treated "which grid cell to search next" — maintain a posterior over fix-location, and at each step greedily open the file maximizing expected entropy reduction of that posterior (not the file with the highest raw relevance score) — these differ whenever relevance is highly correlated across the top candidates (e.g., near-duplicate files), where information-gain probing spends the read budget breaking ties instead of confirming what's already known.

**Deterministic mechanism sketch:** maintain posterior p(file=fix). At each step compute expected information gain EIG(file) = H(p) − E_{outcome}[H(p | opened file)] using a cheap, computable "outcome" proxy (e.g., predicted match/no-match of a stronger signal given the file's content) and open the argmax. Use current entropy H(p) itself as your stopping rule / query-difficulty flag: high residual entropy after k probes = flag as a "hard query" needing broader search, low entropy = safe to stop early — directly actionable for a token-budgeted tool. **Target: active-search.**

### (6)–(8) Free-energy/annealing budgets, Maximum Caliber, general physics-IR (brief)

Free-Energy Machine ([arXiv:2412.09285](https://arxiv.org/html/2412.09285v1)) — mean-field free energy F = ⟨E⟩ − T·S annealed from high to low T — gives SOTA MaxCut/MaxSAT results (448/454 MaxSAT-2016 optimal, 0.074s avg) but is stochastic (Adam + random restarts), not deterministic, and targets graph-partition/SAT, not knapsack-style budgeted subset selection; weak direct fit. "When Knowledge Is Not Free" ([arXiv:2606.02245](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02245)) is the closest applied precedent for your token-budget problem — explicit 0/1-knapsack evidence selection under an access-cost budget, with the finding that *no static selector dominates* and an adaptive/agentic controller beats any fixed heuristic, a useful cautionary data point against over-tuning a fixed heuristic combination. Maximum Caliber ([arXiv:1711.03450](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.03450)) gives the exact multi-hop analogue of MaxEnt: p(path) ∝ q(path)·exp(−γ·F(path)) solved by the same Lagrange-multiplier/partition-function machinery, deterministic given constraints — but has no existing graph-routing/multi-hop-IR instantiation; someone has to build it. No dedicated 2023-2026 "physics-inspired IR" cluster was found beyond the above; GraphRAG-era work (GFMRAG, GNN-RAG) is neural/trained and off-target.

## Top-3 ranked

1. **MaxEnt fusion via Iterative Scaling** — target: **fusion**. Highest confidence: closed-form, provably-convergent, literally designed for "combine several classifiers/channels without training on labels." Directly replaces your hand-tuned weighted sum.
2. **Bayesian/expected-information-gain active search (Scorpion/AF447 lineage + entropy-QPP)** — target: **active-search**. Real operational precedent, deterministic update rule, gives both a next-probe policy and a principled stopping/difficulty signal for the token budget.
3. **Ising/Potts module energy with query field** — target: **within-subsystem**. Mature theory (community detection), the CPM potential-game result gives convergence guarantees for local-move descent, and the field-term trick is a natural, small extension for query-conditioning.

(RG coarse-to-fine is a close 4th — strong conceptual fit and one real precedent (RGMem, tensor-network RG-search) but less directly transplantable machinery than the top 3.)
