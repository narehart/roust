# arXiv Scout Report: Graph-Based & Cold-Start Recommendation for File Localization

## Rank 1 — RP3beta degree-normalization (target: **popularity-bias, within-subsystem**)

**Key result.** RP3beta (Paudel, Christoffel, Newell & Bernstein, "Updatable, Accurate, Diverse, and Scalable Recommendations for Interactive Applications," ACM TiiS 7, 2016; originally RecSys 2016) extends the P3alpha random walk (Cooper, Lee, Radzik & Siantos, "Random Walks in Recommender Systems: Exact Computation and Simulations," WWW 2014). Both do a bipartite/graph random walk (user→item→user→item, or here: file→symbol/import→file) and rank by transition probability; RP3beta adds a **post-hoc division by `degree(item)^β`**. β=0 reduces to plain P3alpha (which itself uses `degree^α` smoothing on the *forward* walk — α<1 flattens hub dominance, α=1 is the raw walk). A 2022 large-scale benchmark (Anelli et al., "Top-N Recommendation Algorithms: A Quest for the State-of-the-Art," UMAP 2022, sisinflab.poliba.it) places RP3beta 3rd in Recall against 15+ classical and neural models, and both EASE-R and RP3beta "outperform others significantly" — with RP3beta requiring **zero training**.

**One-line leap.** Don't just walk the graph — normalize by *destination* popularity, not just source, and tune the exponent as an explicit dial (0 = no penalty, 1 = aggressive long-tail favoring) rather than a fixed heuristic.

**Mechanism sketch for bgrep.** This is a near-exact match to the hand-patched hub-suppression fix already in the retrieval-recipe notes ("mass pools in `__init__.py` hubs and dilutes over distance," global PPR rejected for the same reason). Instead of ad hoc dilution, score each 1-hop/import-graph neighbor candidate `j` reached from a strong hit as `walk_score(i→j) / indegree(j)^β`, with β swept (start at 0.5–1.0, per Anelli's tuned range) rather than hand-tuned per failure. The α exponent on the *forward* edge weight is a second, independent knob — worth exposing separately since bgrep currently conflates "hub suppression" with "distance decay" in one patch. This is the most direct transplant: same math, same failure mode (hub absorbing walk mass), already partially reinvented in bgrep's own 1-hop+RM3 recipe.

Sources: [Cooper et al., WWW 2014 PDF](https://archives.iw3c2.org/www2014/proceedings/companion/p811.pdf), [Paudel et al., ACM TiiS](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2955101), [Anelli et al., UMAP 2022](https://sisinflab.poliba.it/publications/2022/ABDJP22/3503252.3531292.pdf), [step-by-step RP3beta writeup](https://www.stepbystepdatascience.com/rp3beta-python)

## Rank 2 — Content+CF hybrid neighbor blending, "simple beats neural" (target: **within-subsystem, @1**)

**Key result.** Dacrema, Cremonesi & Jannach, "Are We Really Making Much Progress? A Worrying Analysis of Recent Neural Recommendation Approaches" (RecSys 2019, Best Paper; arXiv:1907.06902) found **11 of 12 reproducible neural methods were beaten by simple nearest-neighbor or linear methods**. The specific winner relevant here: **ItemKNN-CFCBF**, a hybrid that concatenates the collaborative-filtering item-item similarity matrix with a content-based (item-feature) similarity matrix before doing k-NN — it "consistently outperformed CVAE" (a deep generative cold-start model) across all tested list lengths. This isn't a new idea dressed up — it's literally weighted-sum neighbor blending, decades old, still winning against 2018-era neural cold-start models when evaluated honestly.

**One-line leap.** Cold items have no CF signal but do have content signal (metadata/text/features) — blend the two similarity matrices *before* ranking rather than trying to learn a joint embedding; the linear blend is a stronger, more robust cold-start bridge than most learned alternatives.

**Mechanism sketch for bgrep.** This is the abstract version of what the retrieval-recipe notes already call "auxiliary channels must be monotone — add candidates, never reorder the lexical head" (design law observed twice: PPR, then commit-message RRF). ItemKNN-CFCBF's lesson generalizes that law: a *fixed-weight linear combination* of a structural-similarity matrix (import graph / co-change) and a content-similarity matrix (BM25 lexical) is empirically hard to beat, provided the combination weight is stable and doesn't let one channel's noise dominate — exactly why raw score-addition/RRF-fusion for the commit-message channel was rejected in bgrep's own log ("history vocabulary reorders the head") while monotone-tail-append worked. The RecSys literature suggests the fix isn't fusion mechanics but where in the ranked list the hybrid signal is allowed to act — consistent with bgrep's tiered-promotion pattern (anchor strength gates top-10 entry, not flat re-ranking).

Sources: [Dacrema et al., RecSys 2019 (ar5iv)](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1907.06902), [ACM DL PDF](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/3298689.3347058?download=true), [GitHub repo w/ full results tables](https://github.com/MaurizioFD/RecSys2019_DeepLearning_Evaluation)

## Rank 3 — Deterministic popularity-bias re-ranking / IPS-style score decay (target: **popularity-bias, feedback-loop**)

**Key result.** Abdollahpouri, Burke & Mobasher, "Managing Popularity Bias in Recommender Systems with Personalized Re-ranking" (arXiv:1901.07555, FLAIRS 2019) propose an xQuAD-derived re-ranking pass with a controllable λ trading off relevance against long-tail exposure, reporting it "manages popularity bias more effectively than an existing regularization-based method" while holding accuracy near-acceptable. This sits alongside classic IPS-weighted ranking (Joachims et al., "Unbiased Learning-to-Rank with Biased Feedback," WSDM 2017 — reweight by inverse propensity to correct for exposure, not relevance) and Abdollahpouri's broader 2023 survey ("A Survey on Popularity Bias in Recommender Systems," arXiv:2308.01118), which frames the fully deterministic version cleanly: at ranking time, divide/decay each candidate's score by a running count of how often it has been retrieved/exposed historically (a deterministic propensity proxy — no learned click model required).

**One-line leap.** Popularity bias correction doesn't require probabilistic click models — a deterministic exposure counter (times-retrieved, or static structural in-degree as a propensity proxy) applied as a divisor at re-rank time gets most of the benefit, and it composes with a diversity re-ranker (xQuAD/MMR-style) that explicitly budgets slots across "aspects" rather than pure top-K by score.

**Mechanism sketch for bgrep.** This directly targets sibling-starvation: give every file a deterministic exposure score (in-degree in the import/co-change graph, or — more powerfully — accumulated cross-query retrieval frequency for the repo, since bgrep already has an incremental per-repo index per the v0.2 notes) and apply a light, capped divisor at the tail-append stage only, never at the head (consistent with the monotone-tail law). This is also where **session-based / stigmergy recommendation** (association rules and Markov chains on click sessions — Ludewig & Jannach, "Evaluation of Session-based Recommendation Algorithms," 2018, showing simple co-occurrence/sequential-rules and V-SKNN beating GRU4Rec on several datasets) becomes directly actionable: bgrep could log which files an agent *opens* after a retrieval bundle (a session), accumulate a co-access count deterministically per repo, and use it exactly as a session-KNN neighbor signal — no training, matches bgrep's zero-learned-parameters constraint, and is the concrete mechanism for "could bgrep accumulate its own usage trails."

Sources: [Abdollahpouri et al., arXiv:1901.07555](https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.07555), [Joachims et al., Unbiased LTR](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/people/tj/publications/joachims_etal_17a.pdf), [Survey on Popularity Bias, arXiv:2308.01118](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.01118), [Session-rec framework / evaluation](https://github.com/rn5l/session-rec)

## Notable but not top-3

- **xQuAD** (Santos, Macdonald & Ounis, "Explicit Search Result Diversification," ECIR 2010) — the general intent-aware diversification framework underlying #3; worth reading directly if bgrep builds an explicit diversity budget across "aspects" (subsystems) rather than score-based re-ranking.
- **Knowledge-graph path/meta-path methods (PathSim, non-learned)** — theoretically the closest analog to bgrep's import graph, but the literature confirms these require hand-crafted meta-paths and *still* underperform on cold-start without a learned component (MetaKG, arXiv:2202.03851) — low transfer value, skip.
- The RP3beta and P3alpha degree-exponent formalism (#1) and the ItemKNN-CFCBF hybrid-blend result (#2) are the two highest-confidence transplants because bgrep's own campaign log independently rediscovered both mechanisms (hub-dilution suppression, monotone auxiliary-channel fusion) without the formal vocabulary — the RecSys literature offers tuned exponent ranges and a citable "why this generalizes" argument rather than a new idea.
